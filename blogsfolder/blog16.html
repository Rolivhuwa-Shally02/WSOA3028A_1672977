Radical AI Podcast: Love, Challenge and Hope: Building a Movement to Dismantle the New Jim Code :Reflection

June 20, 2021 by Mandaha RS


This is a review on an interview about ethical issues, racism, discrimination integrating with technology, asking questions such as, Is the AI racist, is technology racist? Also involving
a research on how racism is emended in technology system that led to writing of a book by Dr Ruha Benjamin called Race After Technology which addressed
and discussed this issue, we first need to understand this concept of racism, which means discriminating or an act of treating a group people
unequally because of race, colour or ethnic group. For a product or system to be racist we must ask ourselves if it means any harm to people, was it intentional because
not everything that seem racist was indeed made to be racist.
Dr Benjamin investigated other issues as dimensions of science, medicine and technology while focusing on relationship between innovation, inequality, health justice, knowledge
and power which all affect the society as a whole and growth. She gave her view of race being a technology itself by stratifying social injustice in the architecture, analysing this
will make one question the technologies that are being sold and the ones we make ourselves, whether every individual form any race of any colour can use those specific tools.
My view is that addressing this, is only important if laws, policies, industries, fields consider racism as important to the society, we also need to know what to do as an
individual in the fields we work in, our practices and products we develop that they don’t appear as supporting racism furthermore products, technology tools being sold
should account for all races, its not about making something that will “wow” consumers but it should consider local,
national organisation pushing at the policy level.

Some policies for workers were not reviewed thoroughly for black workers that later these workers get affected, for an example some policies made are affecting workers
during the pandemic all because of technology associated systems, In hospitals there are machines that now sort pills, they offer food to patients, or other machines for
certain purposes and later we find that they affect communication, black people having power and we mostly fear black people losing jobs because mostly that’s the case and
already they would be struggling financially. Another example is having drug penalty higher for black people but lower for white people as this was addressed
by Dr Ruha. Some technology tools are made to work better on particular skin colour, like form of a research conducted on laser treatment causing many issues on people
with dark skin meanwhile the effects on white skin were not really identified but only common side effects, the reason
could be that the manufacturer is of white colour and this treatment was
not tested on a person with dark skin.


I certainly agree that sometimes an approach to technology for solutions is not necessary, if the change benefits people of a particular race and not the other then that is racism,
if certain products are made available to everyone but not meant for every race that plays a role in racism. Another simple example of
an incident of hair products that were being sold at Clicks store which were not considering natural black hair but they were made available for everyone so that
is another issue of racism, point is if you are selling an item, or product out there to everyone then that product/item should be usable by everybody, the benefit should be
on the seller and both ethnic groups. It is also said that love plays a role, if you have love for all people, of all colour or race, indeed your energy and your skills will
be used not to harm but to better the lives of the communities. We must also use our voice to make a change, identify and address what needs to be changed
in technology tools that are racist, what needs to be changed in workplace, faculty of study,
field of study/work must be addressed loudly to bring a change.
