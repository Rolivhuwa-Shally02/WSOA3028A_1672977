Digital Divide

May 20, 2021 by Mandaha RS


Digital divide is the inequality between those who benefit and those who do not digitally, that means access to technology and software tools.
The gap between developed regions and non- developed regions, poor and rich people. It also has to do with
economic and social inequality. The nature of divide is considered in terms of digital devices and subscriptions.
People from rural areas and those have no money or education cannot have access to more communication
through devices and thus they tend to lack information about what is happening globally or even know or understand
about digital divide. There are three factors which affect digital divide namely, information accessibility,
information utilisation and information receptiveness.
Looking back into being educated and aware of how to use technology devices, this plays a role in accessing information because one needs to know how to use
the internet, the web and all other software applications. Connectivity also falls under accessibility, having
access to the internet at home, schools, workplace and other locations is important. Levels of connectivity
varies in different areas, the connectivity in the villages will not be proper like in the cities, this is
something that I have noticed myself, apart from facing internet disruption due to load shedding, internet
connection in the rural areas can be very poor.

The inequality might be due to demographic and socio-economic characteristics; income, level of education, gender, race, areas of living, skills that one has, political,
cultural and economic aspects. Research has shown that people living in urban areas have more access, then
when it comes to digital and IT information, males know more on that and mostly employed in that industry.
Cultural factor limits people based on their class or status, education level and background from producing content,
like in YouTube, Facebook, Instagram and most times is because people do no have items and materials for such.

How to compensate Digital divide


The main question of how to address the digital divide is closely related with the question of what specific benefit is expected with ICTs. Only with the
answer to this question in mind can the digital divide meaningfully be bridged in a way that leads to real-world positive impact.
A person must be able to connect in order to achieve enhancement of social and cultural capital as well as achieve mass economic gains
in productivity. Accessibility is a necessary condition for overcoming the digital divide. Access to ICT meets significant challenges that stem from income
restrictions. Follow up on what is happening in the country, economically, politically and other important aspects that
keep you alert of essential information. Get technical help on how to use technology tools, how they
can benefit you in the working industry as well as education wise or salary wise.
