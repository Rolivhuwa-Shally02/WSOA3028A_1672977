Experts worry about your Personal data! : Reflection

June 09, 2021 by Mandaha RS


In April 2018, a well-known social network made headlines when it was discovered that private information of users was accessed by Cambridge Analytica for advertisement
purposes during United States election in 2016. Cambridge Analytica is British political data-analysis firm that
is no longer in action. Facebook disclosed about 87 million users’ data without their knowledge and this was reported
to be a security breach tied to Cambridge Analytica following Trump’s 2016 elections.
Facebook Inc. Chief Executive Officer Mark Zuckerberg
admitted to the reports however he was not
aware of such and apologised for not protecting users’ information as he should have. Further investigations were done and later in September Facebook admitted to have
leaked 50 million users’ data to cybercriminals and they later on revealed that it was due to a security bug which
allowed access to 6.8 million users’ profiles. People’s information was accessed by hackers, no one knows what they could
do with so much information about a person and that puts people lives at risk. More information and ethical
issues such as weak cyber security, honesty, reliability etc will be discussed in the report.
Furthermore, later in September 2018, Facebook announced the existence of a security bug that allowed access to cybercriminals in obtaining data of 50 million users
and after that in December they revealed that the security failure allowed unauthorised access to profiles of
6.8 million users. Following the breakout news more information was released, Cambridge Analytica suspended the
CEO of the company and Mark Zuckerberg published a post apologizing to their involvement in Facebook data
breach and not protecting users against the abuse of personal information. Facebook was given a 5
billion penalty by FTC (Federal Trade Commission) for violating users’ privacy.
The social platform’s vice president Ime Archibong stated that they are working on making changes by fixing the product’s failures and building new higher standards
products under the requirements given out by the FTC. The requirements included that Facebook must block third-party
applications. However there were speculations that the information that was harvested was still in use for electoral
purposes in 2020 but Donald Trump still did not win the elections and that relived people, for this is not the
only case where data is being harvested, have control of which application wants to capture your personal data
where possible, do not just accept cookies or using your google account
to sign in every application.
