Methods for analysis and critiques of UX and/or UI

April 05, 2021 by Mandaha RS


For a start it is important to know that UX and UI have to do with design, they work together and in other means they
complement each other. UX which stands User Experience is the process of developing and improving
quality of interaction between the user and the product. According to Don Norman “UX encompasses all
aspects of the end-users’ interaction with the company, its services and products”, therefore
it is employed across products, interfaces and services. It was adopted by the Human-Computer
Interaction (HCI), community practitioners and researchers, Ideas represented by UX are important but no means
original-Mark Hassenzahl and Noam Tractinsky. UX has to do with data, analysis, flows, research, testing,
interaction and usability it is about putting all UI elements together in one product,
how to put them together, how you experience or that product or service.
UI stands for User Interface which has to do with the look and feel of the product and it is about visualisation, using images, colours, videos,
typography, spacing, proximity, consistency, etc. It combines Visual Design and Interaction Design, it makes interfaces
beautiful and readable, it makes emotional connections. An example could be from the technology devices we use on daily
basis like phones, laptops, tablets in terms of how we input data to interact with them, touching on the screen or using keyboard
on our laptops. Both UI and UX work together to create user-friendly and aesthetically rich products however UX is done first
and then UI follows. The role of UX design is to work on process and strategy (product structure and analysis,
content development, etc.), Wireframing and prototyping, Execution and Analytics so basically
that is what a UX designer focuses on. A UI designer works on the look and feel of the product and responsiveness and interactivity.
Looking at the common methods of UX or UI, we have persona and storytelling, sketch, wireframe and prototype, User Test and interviews, Usability
test and scenario, pattern library and Heuristic analysis however some of these methods were mentioned above
as roles of these designs. Methods of analysis of UX and UI in particular are Website and Device Analytics
(information about click paths, target demographics and device preferences), Business and sales analytics (allow companies to measure their success)
, Competitor Benchmarking (surface opportunities for improvement), UX principles Benchmarking (evaluates website/product against principles),
UX checklists and Heuristics (Analyse and evaluates on how to improve design), UX Trends and Evaluation
(Identifies new direction in the field of UX), Technical Analysis (check for constraints and limitations), Process Analytics
(understanding how the system works).
The question could be why is UI and UX important in design? well it is important that the design of the product makes sense to users, it should meet
the user’s needs, they must be able to use that product, use simple language and design with accessibility.
There are several benefits of these designs such as improving user’s acquisition, Keeping the user’s on using the product,
effective product and well designed with no bugs, Increase in productivity and lastly more user engagement. UX
on its own is useful in creating products of value, increase trust leading to increase in profit,
and help in reduction of long-term costs (reduction in customer support and errors). References
