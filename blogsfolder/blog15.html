Making a feminist internet in Africa: why the internet needs African feminists and feminism :Reflection

June 13, 2021 by Mandaha RS


I would first like to give an overview of feminism then I will later give a discussion of my perspective on this context. Feminism can be the advocacy of women’s
rights concerning equality of gender or it could mean the political, economic and social equality of gender. Feminism
is shaped by the issues faced across the nation, the struggles because of race and gender inequality even here in Africa.
gatherings were conducted in April 2014 to explore internet feminism, Pointing out changes in feminist analysis
and the purpose of internet and technology in our daily lives.
The research shows that there have been changes in years 2015 and 2019, there has been an increase in internet users by a rate of 15 million with social media being the
highest influencer of the change, I would not disagree on this because through technology devices we have been
stimulated to let our voices out in social media, to communicate through Facebook, Twitter, Instagram, YouTube
and other social platforms, there are many feminists making their voice to be heard and addressing struggles we face as women
and more as black females through social media because that is where most people spend their days on, on their
devices, that is where we access information looking at how times are changing.
Africa as whole does not have more access to the internet, technology devices that are modified and connectivity so that is a matter of inequality of access to and
representation on the internet, Well Africa has resources of materials wanted by more developed countries thus they will use this
region for experiments, testing drugs \ illegally and this is because there do not have a voice over social
media to address the issues or expose these acts, sometimes it’s because the regions are poor
and they even lack information about things.
The use of internet contributed to growing and making Feminists in Africa, connecting them and using the power of internet to address more issues we face based on inequality.
These gatherings and meetings can take over the internet and social media if indeed they are meant to build and not divide,
there is no form of violent conveyed in any way so for that if the change will uplift and empower women
in Africa the indeed more voices are needed to push economic, social, cultural and political
aspects to better standard.
